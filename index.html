Script Analysis: The Weekend

Look at how Neil talks about his weekend.

"I go / went to a museum... and I do / did some other interesting things too."

We use the Past Simple for finished actions. What is the past of "go"?

Grammar Engine: Past Simple

To talk about finished events (like last weekend), we use irregular past verbs.

Present

I go

I do

I see

Past

I went

I did

I saw

Make a sentence: "Last weekend, I went to..."

Script Analysis: Past Habits

Neil talks about when his children were younger.

"I use / used to go on lots of day trips with my kids."

What does "used to" mean? Does he still do it now?

Grammar Engine: "Used To"

We use Used to + Base Verb for things that were true or habits in the past, but are NOT true now.

Past Habit

"I used to like theme parks."

(In the past: Yes. Now: No).

Negative Form

"I didn't use to like coffee."

(Notice: no "d" in use!).

What is a food you "used to" hate when you were a child, but you like now?

Script Analysis: Desires

How does Beth say she wants to go to Brighton?

"I will / 'd really like to go there on a day trip."

"I'd" is short for "I would". "I would like to" is a polite way to say "I want to".
